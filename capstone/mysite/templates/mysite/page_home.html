Ramesses II

Ramesses the Great
Hover On Image

Ramesses II, commonly known as Ramesses the Great, was one of ancient Egypt's most powerful and celebrated pharaohs, reigning from 1279 to 1213 BCE and known for his military prowess, monumental architecture, and long reign. He was born around 1303 BCE to Seti I and Queen Tuya. He became the third pharaoh of the Nineteenth Dynasty and ascended to the throne around 1279 BCE, likely after serving as co-regent with his father. His reign lasted an impressive 66 years, making it one of the longest in ancient Egyptian history.
